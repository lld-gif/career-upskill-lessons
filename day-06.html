DAY 06

GTM Strategy

Go-to-market planning, ICP definition, and channel strategy.

Learning Objectives

  • Build an Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) from first principles
  • Choose between PLG, sales-led, and hybrid GTM motions
  • Design a channel strategy matched to your market
  • Understand the economics behind GTM decisions (CAC, LTV, payback)

Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Every GTM strategy begins with a clear ICP. Selling to the wrong customers is the fastest way to burn cash and demoralize a sales team.

ICP Components

FirmographicsIndustry, size, revenue, geographyB2B SaaS, 200–2000 employees, $20M–$200M revenue, US/EU
TechnographicsTech stack, tools, maturityUses AWS, has a data team, runs Snowflake

The Anti-ICP

Equally important — define who you should not sell to:

  • Companies too small to afford your product (high churn risk)
  • Industries with regulatory requirements you can't meet
  • Orgs without the technical maturity to implement your solution
  • Prospects where your product solves a nice-to-have, not a must-have
ICP discipline: The best early-stage companies say no to 70%+ of inbound interest. Every bad-fit customer consumes support resources, creates feature requests that distort the roadmap, and churns — destroying LTV.

GTM Motions

Product-Led Growth (PLG)

The product is the primary acquisition, conversion, and expansion vehicle.

  • Model: Free tier or trial → self-serve conversion → team expansion
  • Fits when: Low ACV ($0–$10K), individual user can get value alone, viral/network effects
  • Examples: Slack, Figma, Notion, Datadog
  • Key metrics: Sign-up to activation rate, time-to-value, expansion revenue, NRR

Sales-Led Growth (SLG)

Sales team drives acquisition through outbound and inbound pipeline.

  • Model: SDR/BDR prospecting → AE demos → pilot/POC → contract
  • Fits when: High ACV ($50K+), complex buying committee, requires customization
  • Examples: Snowflake, Palantir, ServiceNow
  • Key metrics: Pipeline coverage, win rate, ACV, sales cycle length, CAC

Hybrid / Product-Led Sales

PLG creates the bottom of funnel; sales converts and expands the best accounts.

  • Model: Free/trial sign-up → product usage signals → sales outreach to high-PQL accounts
  • Fits when: Mid-range ACV ($10K–$100K), land-and-expand opportunity
  • Examples: Atlassian, MongoDB, Twilio
  • Key metrics: PQL-to-SQL conversion rate, expansion ARR, usage-to-contract correlation

GTM Economics

The Unit Economics Triangle

Three numbers govern GTM sustainability:

  • CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): Total S&M spend ÷ new customers acquired
  • LTV (Lifetime Value): Average revenue per customer × gross margin × average lifespan
  • Payback Period: CAC ÷ (Annual revenue per customer × gross margin)

Benchmark Ratios

MetricHealthyGoodGreat
LTV:CAC3:14:15:1+
CAC Payback<24 months<18 months<12 months
Net Revenue Retention>100%>110%>130%
S&M as % of Revenue<50%<40%<30%
The magic of NRR: Net Revenue Retention above 100% means your existing customers are growing faster than they're churning. At 130% NRR, you could stop all new sales and still grow 30% from the installed base. This is the most powerful GTM metric and the one investors scrutinize most.

Channel Strategy

Direct Channels

  • Outbound sales: SDR/AE team targets ICP accounts directly. Highest control, highest cost.
  • Inbound marketing: Content, SEO, paid ads drive leads. Lower cost per lead but requires time to build.
  • Self-serve / PLG: Product drives acquisition. Lowest CAC but requires product investment.

Indirect Channels

  • Technology partnerships: Integrations and marketplace listings (AWS Marketplace, Salesforce AppExchange)
  • Resellers / VARs: Channel partners sell on your behalf (important in government and enterprise)
  • System integrators: SIs implement your product as part of larger engagements (Accenture, Deloitte)
  • Referral programs: Customers and partners refer new business for fees or credits

Channel Economics

Every channel has different unit economics. The right mix depends on ACV, market maturity, and competitive dynamics:

Self-Check Questions

  1. You're launching a cloud cost optimization tool for mid-market companies ($20M–$200M revenue). Define the ICP and recommend a GTM motion.
  2. A company has 25% CAC payback in 8 months but NRR of 95%. What does this tell you? What would you change?
  3. Explain why a company with $5K ACV shouldn't hire an enterprise sales team. What's the alternative?
  4. Design a land-and-expand strategy for a data analytics platform. What's the "land" (first use case) and what drives the "expand"?
  5. When should a startup invest in channel partnerships vs. direct sales? What are the trade-offs?
