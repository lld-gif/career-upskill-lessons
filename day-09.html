Defense Tech Landscape

Defense primes, new entrants, procurement cycles, and ITAR/EAR basics.

The New Entrants (Neoscalers)

A wave of venture-backed defense technology companies has emerged, challenging primes with software-defined approaches:

  • Anduril: Autonomous systems, sensor fusion, Lattice OS platform
  • Palantir: Data integration and AI/ML for defense and intelligence
  • Shield AI: Autonomous aircraft, Hivemind AI pilot
  • Rebellion Defense: AI/ML for mission planning and simulation
  • Hadrian: Automated manufacturing for defense hardware
The thesis: Defense primes are optimized for cost-plus contracts and hardware programs. New entrants are bringing Silicon Valley speed, software-first architectures, and commercial best practices to defense — and increasingly winning programs.

Mid-Tier Defense

Companies between $1B–$15B in defense revenue that often specialize:

  • L3Harris: Communications, ISR, space
  • Leidos: IT services, digital modernization, health
  • SAIC: IT services, engineering, mission support
  • BAE Systems (US): Electronic systems, platforms, cyber
  • Booz Allen Hamilton: Consulting, analytics, cyber, AI

DoD Procurement

The Budget Cycle

Understanding the Planning, Programming, Budgeting, and Execution (PPBE) cycle is critical for timing your sales efforts:

  1. Planning (2–3 years out): DoD sets strategic priorities and capability gaps
  2. Programming (1–2 years out): Services allocate resources to programs
  3. Budgeting (current + 1 year): OMB reviews, President submits budget to Congress
  4. Execution (current year): Funds are obligated and spent. Use-it-or-lose-it pressure in Q4 (July–September)

Acquisition Pathways for Tech Companies

  • SBIR/STTR: Small Business Innovation Research — grants for R&D. Phase I (~$150K), Phase II (~$1M), Phase III (production contract, unlimited).
  • OTA (Other Transaction Authority): Faster, more flexible than FAR-based contracts. Used by DIU, AFWERX, NavalX. Ideal for commercial tech companies.
  • DIU (Defense Innovation Unit): Silicon Valley-based DoD org that connects commercial tech to military needs. Prototypes in 12–24 months.
  • AFWERX / NavalX / Army Futures: Service-specific innovation organizations with streamlined procurement.
  • GSA Schedule: Pre-negotiated contract vehicle — once on schedule, any federal agency can buy from you.
The OTA advantage: Other Transaction Authorities bypass most FAR (Federal Acquisition Regulation) requirements, meaning no cost accounting system requirements, faster award timelines (months vs. years), and commercial-friendly terms. This is the primary entry point for tech companies entering defense.

Export Controls: ITAR & EAR

ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulations)

Governs defense articles and services on the United States Munitions List (USML):

  • Controlled by the State Department (DDTC)
  • Covers military-specific items: weapons, military aircraft, satellites, crypto for military use
  • Export (including sharing with foreign nationals in the US) requires a license
  • Violations carry severe penalties — criminal prosecution, massive fines, debarment
  • US persons only can access ITAR data — this drives hiring and facility requirements

EAR (Export Administration Regulations)

Governs dual-use items on the Commerce Control List (CCL):

  • Controlled by the Commerce Department (BIS)
  • Covers commercial items with military applications: semiconductors, encryption, certain software
  • Less restrictive than ITAR but still requires compliance
  • Export to certain countries/entities is restricted or prohibited

Practical Implications

  • Hiring: ITAR-controlled programs require US Person employees (citizens and permanent residents)
  • Cloud infrastructure: ITAR data must be stored in US-only, access-controlled environments (GovCloud, IL4/IL5)
  • Product development: If your product incorporates ITAR data, the entire product may become ITAR-controlled
  • Open source: Generally exempt from export controls under the "publicly available" exclusion, but be careful with military-specific implementations
